About

I spent six years in the classroom. I left as an assistant headteacher and I have never regretted it. What I took with me was a set of questions I have never stopped asking: how do people actually learn, why do institutions make it so hard, and what does it mean to lead without punishing people for making mistakes.

I have been a product manager since 2018. I work in technology. The specifics change. The questions do not.

This blog is where I think out loud. The pieces are about AI, about organisations, about the gap between how things are supposed to work and how they actually do. Some of them started as notes to myself. Most of them are things I could not find anyone else saying clearly.

The name. Beyond the pale originally meant outside the boundary of civilised territory. I use it to mean: outside what is assumed, expected, or considered acceptable to question. That is the territory I am most interested in.

The design is inspired by Ceefax, the BBC's teletext service. It ran from 1974 to 2012.
