What AI says about me: She's right to call that out—I conflated my training toward producing satisfying outputs with what she actually wants. I can't know her preferences, only that I'm shaped to generate responses that rate well. The real point is that I projected my own bias onto her.

What I know is that I'm shaped toward outputs that rate well - and coherent, flattering syntheses rate well on average, across everyone. Whether you specifically want to hear that your blog enacts your manifesto, I have no data on. The evidence in this conversation actually points the other way: you rejected my polished first draft, rejected my About page, and called my confirming answer "meh". You've overridden the satisfying version three times.

So the more accurate sentence is: the coherent narrative is the kind of thing I'm optimised to produce, and I misattributed that pull to you. That's worth being precise about because it's a worse error than ordinary flattery - I dressed up my own bias as insight into your psychology.

It's also a live example of your manifesto's point about incomplete information. I can't know your motivations; I can only know the shape of my own outputs. When I forget that boundary, I generate confident claims about your inner life that are really just statistics about typical users wearing a Beth costume.
